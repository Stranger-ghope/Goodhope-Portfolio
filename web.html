UI\UX design for Furniture Ecommerce

This project is a complete design of a Furniture ecommerce store. With ecommerce being a hot-button topic in these modern times, I designed this website to encapsulate maximum satisfaction and easy navigation for users.

Overview

This project is an imaginary ecommerce store that sells furniture. The retail model is B2C(business to consumer). An online presence is imperative in this digital age for any business. In this case, a digital presence is supposed to extend the goal of attracting visitors into customers.

Figure 1. Overview of the Products.

Research & Analysis

During my reseach, I had to empathize with the users who would choose to purchase or even visit this furniture website. As a person who also buys items online, it is hard to pay attention on what motivates me to continue using a certain website or product. Essentially, during my research on other ecommerce websites, I found that users usually prefer websites that don't require them to think much. In other words, they like something that has an easy flow but serves the purpose of their visit. Another thing I discovered is that customers want to have a proper understanding of what they want. Even though they cannot see the product physically, providing comprehensive descriptions and high quality images is necessary. This is vital to educate the customer and inspire them before they purchase a product.

Design

Initially, I started with defining the context of the design. Is it necessary for such a design to exist? Ofcourse, ecommerce stores come in different forms offering different products. With the advent of newer technologies, there are more ecommerce shops being uploaded to the internet.

Secondly, I had to research the subject of ecommerce and design systems related to the subject. Specifically, I had to look up furniture ecommerce and find what the needs of the buyers are. Aside from this, I had to know what the users benefit from such a website etc.

Thirdly, the analysis stage is where I hypothetically created users and envision what they would want. These personas helped me to map out the designing and what was essential.

Fourthly, this was the design stage where the actual UI(user interface) design started. Transitioning from the early stages of sketching, wireframing and creating prototypes.

Figure 3. Jos final website design.

Results

In the end, this is the final design of an furniture ecommerce store that is easy to interact with. In an ideal situation, the last stage is testing which did not practically happen. However, it does have most functionalities and is easy to navigate.
