Partial UI redesign of IOS Anchor podcast app

This is a partial redesign of the IOS Anchor podcast app. The redesign mainly focuses on improving the user interface and experience of podcasters.

Overview

Anchor is a free podcasting app provided by spotify. It is easy to use and convenient as it doesn't require a lot of expertise.

Problem

As a an easy-to-use app, it provides what users and fulfils their purpose. Podcasters and listeners can both fulfill their goal. After reading the reviews on appstore, I noticed the similar reviews from users. They appreciated a "community" feature which allows podcasters to interact with their listeners. According to one review, the process of finding episodes with replies was tedious. The podcaster had to manually tap on every podcast to know whether there was a reply in the question and answers section. Another review pointed out that the editing tools were not goood enough. In this case, I only focused on the redesign of the community feature. I decided to redesign the app and add one more destination in the navigation bar. This new additional nav icon is for all the Q&A replies between podcaster and listeners. Instead of having everything cluttered, I thought it would be better to have it this way. Most importanty, the community Q&A replies will filter read from unread replies. Making it easier for the user to go through replies with the aid of notifications.

Figure 1. Anchor IOS application before and after redesign.

Research & Analysis

User research for this redesign was based on reading the reviews as I mentioned earlier. This was the main method used to evaluate the needs of the users pertaining to the community feature. It is reliable to get feedback from reviews because the user's complaint is well articulated and experiential. Additionally, It means they have been using the app long enough to notice the problem.

The objective was to eliminate the manual method of finding podcast episodes with replies. Simply sorting all the unread replies was what I came up with.

Wireframing & Prototyping

Since this was not a full app redesign, the wireframing was not a difficult task. I still had to draw some wireframes to contexualize what was needed.

Figure 2. Anchor podcast IOS app low-fi wireframes.

Design

The first step was empathy with the user to understand how they felt. I understood how difficult and disorganized it was to scroll through an endless list of episodes without a notification.

Secondly, I defined what was needed to fix the problem that the users were facing. On this stage, I just had to clearly know what the problem was to transition to the next stage.

Ideation was the next stage which mainly focused on solutions and brainstorming. Evaluating different ways that can help solve the problem users were facing.

Lastly, the actual design was what I did. Starting with sketches, wireframes and Prototyping.

Figure 3. Anchor podcast IOS application redesign.

Results

In the results, I noticed that this would be an easier way for podcasters to view replies. However, there was not practical test with users.
